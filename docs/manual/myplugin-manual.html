What My Plugin does

My Plugin is a volume tool. It takes your mono signal, adjusts its level with a single GAIN knob — from a whisper 24 dB quieter to a roar 24 dB louder — and sends it to both sides of a stereo output.

Use it to balance one instrument against another, to push a quiet synth up in front of a band, or as a clean boost anywhere in your chain.

Good to know: My Plugin is completely clean — it changes nothing about your tone, only the level, at any knob setting.

Quick start

  1. Add My Plugin to your pedalboard. In the MOD web interface, find it under the brand myplugin and drag it into your signal chain.
  2. Play. At the default (noon) the plugin passes your signal through at exactly the level it came in.
  3. Turn GAIN right to boost, left to cut, until it sits where you want it in the mix.

The controls

1 GAIN

How loud the output is compared to the input.

  • Left (−24 dB): a distant whisper
  • Noon (0 dB, default): exactly as loud as what comes in
  • Right (+24 dB): a big, clean boost
Stereo out of a mono pedal? My Plugin sends the same signal to left and right, so it slots cleanly in front of stereo effects like reverb, delay, or chorus.

Installing My Plugin

Download the build for your platform from the plugin's GitHub Releases page — each release ships a MOD Desktop bundle (myplugin-vX.Y.Z-linux-x86_64.tar.gz) and a MOD Dwarf bundle (myplugin-vX.Y.Z-dwarf-aarch64.tar.gz).

MOD Desktop (Linux)

  1. Extract the archive: tar xzf myplugin-vX.Y.Z-linux-x86_64.tar.gz
  2. Move the extracted myplugin.lv2 folder into ~/Documents/MOD Desktop/lv2/
  3. Restart MOD Desktop. My Plugin appears in the plugin library under the brand myplugin.

MOD Dwarf

  1. Connect the Dwarf over USB and extract the Dwarf archive on your computer.
  2. Copy the bundle onto the unit: 
    scp -O -r myplugin.lv2 root@192.168.51.1:/root/.lv2/
  3. Restart the audio services (or simply reboot the Dwarf): 
    ssh root@192.168.51.1 'systemctl restart jack2 mod-ui'
  4. Hard-refresh the MOD web interface in your browser (Ctrl-Shift-R) and drag My Plugin onto a pedalboard.

Questions & answers

I hear nothing after installing.
Make sure the pedal is connected on the pedalboard — the input jack on its left, both output jacks on its right — and that GAIN is not fully left. On the Dwarf, restart jack2 and mod-ui (or reboot) after copying the bundle.
Does the boost distort?
My Plugin itself stays clean at any setting, but a big boost can push the next pedal — or the output stage — into clipping. If you hear crackling, back GAIN off or lower the level further down the chain.
